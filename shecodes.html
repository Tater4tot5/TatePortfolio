Branding and Logo Design

At the time of this project, the goodreads logo was just the name "Goodreads" written in a plain, sans-serif font with little personality. I wanted to create an updated logo that would still have a refined look, but be a play on the brand's main subjects: reading and books. I also wanted to incorporate text and colors that evoked a sense of being at a library and viewing actual print books, so I chose a serif font and muted colors for the body of the page.

Sketches and Wireframes

I sketched out my ideas with mobile-first design in mind. I chose to make the local, side navigation sticky so that it stayed with users as they scrolled through their library, and kept the key, global navigation at the top, hiding only the local navigation in mobile view. I created realistic wireframes in Photoshop.

Challenges and Takeaways

In my original sketches, I wanted a slider at the top of the page showcasing books from each category (inspired by Netflix and Amazon). However, after reconsidering the user experience, I realized a slider would be unncessary because Goodreads users would be visiting the "My Books" page to add and sort books they've already read, not to discover new books. I encountered several roadblocks and challenges when it came to coding my design. Ideally, I would have had a prominent search bar located at the top of the page just below the navigation. Given more time, I would have implemented this with a javascript plugin.
